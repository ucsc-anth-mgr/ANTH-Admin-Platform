Cycle deadlines

The two dates a cycle's schedule is computed from, chosen once per academic year from the department calendar. Everything else — drafts due, deliberation, the vote, the letter, and the candidate review windows — is worked out backward from the division deadline (and, for promotions, forward from the letters-due date). We store the calendar's entry, not a copy of the date, so if the date moves upstream the schedule follows.

Schedule spacing

How much time each step of the review needs, in business days (weekends and campus closures are skipped automatically). The candidate review window has a policy basis; the rest is the department's own process — tune it here and every schedule recomputes.
